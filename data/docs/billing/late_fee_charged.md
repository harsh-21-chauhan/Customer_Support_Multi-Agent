# Late fee charged

## Category
billing

## Problem
You have been charged a late fee on your account, but you are unsure why it was applied or how to avoid it in the future. 

## Solution
1. Check your payment history to ensure that your payment method is up-to-date and that there are no failed payments.
2. Verify that your billing information is accurate, including your billing address and payment method details.
3. If you have an overdue invoice, settle the outstanding balance as soon as possible to prevent additional late fees.
4. Review your account settings to confirm that your payment method is set to auto-pay, which can help prevent late payments.
5. If you are still unsure about the late fee, contact our support team with your account details and a description of the issue, and we will investigate further.


## Notes
Late fees are automatically applied to accounts with overdue invoices. The amount of the late fee is a percentage of the outstanding balance, as specified in our terms of service. If you are experiencing financial difficulties or have extenuating circumstances that have caused the late payment, please contact our support team to discuss possible alternatives. Please note that we cannot waive late fees for payments that are more than 30 days overdue.
